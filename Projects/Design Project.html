Design of Disk Sorting Machine

Designed a gravity-powered sorting machine performing all mechanical calculations that were validated through simulation

Project Description

As part of the Mechanical Engineering Design course at the Lebanese American University, I co-designed and built a fully mechanical sorting machine to classify 69 steel disks (60 mm, 100 mm, 150 mm in diameter) based purely on size, without using any sensors or electronics. The design leverages gravity, a sloped feeding chute, a thickness gate, and a motor-driven rotating shaft system with progressively increasing shaft gaps to direct each disk into the correct collection box. All mechanical calculations—bearing loads, shaft stresses, fatigue life, buckling, and torque requirements—were performed by hand and thoroughly verified using SolidWorks simulations and stress analysis. The final design ensures structural integrity, high safety margins, and long-term durability, making it a robust, low-cost solution for industrial sorting applications.
